The Correlation
Since GPT-3's release in June 2020, AI training compute has scaled ~1600× in 5 years, with models now trained at the 5×10²⁶ FLOPS scale. During this same period, expert estimates of P(doom) have risen from ~2% to ~12%.
The Exponential Curve
Training compute doubles every 5.6 months on average. GPT-4 (March 2023) used 68× more compute than GPT-3. GPT-5 (August 2025) uses 23× more than GPT-4. This exponential trajectory shows no signs of slowing: 16 frontier models from OpenAI, Google, Meta, and Anthropic tracked between 2018-2025.
Why P(doom) Increases
Each order of magnitude in compute brings capabilities closer to transformative AI. The jump from GPT-3 (3×10²³) to GPT-4 (2×10²⁵) enabled professional-level reasoning. The next 10× jump to 10²⁶+ FLOPS may cross critical capability thresholds with insufficient safety guarantees. P(doom) estimates reflect compounding uncertainty as we approach AGI without solved alignment.
The 2027 Projection
If compute scaling continues at current rates, models trained at 10²⁷-10²⁸ FLOPS become feasible by 2027. At these scales, many researchers expect human-level AGI becomes probable. Without fundamental breakthroughs in AI safety, P(doom) estimates may reach 20-30% by 2027—a coin flip with extra steps.
